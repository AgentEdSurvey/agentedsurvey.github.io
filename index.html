Abstract

Large language model agents are increasingly used to decompose complex tasks, simulate social interaction, and support educational or psychological decision making. Yet studies at the intersection of multi-agent LLM systems, psychology, behavior, and education remain dispersed across multiple research communities.

This survey reviews 101 studies identified through a systematic search, including 56 journal articles and 45 conference papers. We organize the literature around four application areas: psychological and mental-health assessment, behavioral and social simulation, education and learning support, and human-agent collaboration.

We further synthesize five recurring architectural families and a process-level framework that links role initialization, orchestration, grounding and memory, critique or reflection, consensus, and human validation. We conclude with recommendations for theory-grounded agent design, interaction-level evaluation, reproducible reporting, and human oversight in high-stakes settings.
